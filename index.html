Seville

Dec 12–16 · 4 nights · H10 Casa de la Plata

Royal palaces, tapas streets, and the best Christmas lights in Spain — all walkable from a central hotel. The pace: sightseeing in the morning, long lunch, rest, then evening lights and tapas.

Ronda

Dec 20–23 · 3 nights

Perched above the El Tajo gorge and famous for wine, mushrooms, and pastry. Three nights at a relaxed pace: the town itself, a white-village day trip, and a winery afternoon.

Arrival day — the scenic route Sat Dec 20

Skip the fast highway and drive through Sierra de Grazalema Natural Park:

Cádiz → Arcos de la Frontera (optional) → Grazalema → Ronda

  • Mirador Puerto de las Palomas — panoramic mountain views
  • Grazalema — the lunch stop: payoyo cheese, Iberian pork, lamb, seasonal mushrooms, local wine
  • Zahara de la Sierra — optional stop over the turquoise reservoir

Arrive in Ronda around 4–5 PM, check in, sunset walk across Puente Nuevo, dinner in town.

Where to stay deciding

1 · Catalonia Ronda — recommended

  • Cheapest of the serious options; ~$33 upcharge for kids
  • Modern rooms, elevator, predictable comfort — easiest with a child
  • Next to the Plaza de Toros; the best rooftop bar in town for after bedtime
  • Trade-off: less historic charm

2 · Parador de Ronda

  • Iconic location with spectacular gorge and bridge views
  • Inside the restricted traffic zone — must send car registration to the hotel in advance

3 · Catalonia Reina Victoria

  • Spacious modern rooms, gardens, amazing gorge views, easy paid on-site parking
  • ~10-minute easy walk to the bridge; much easier with a stroller than deep old town
  • Quirk: sink sits outside the bathroom in standard rooms; only suites have fully separated bathrooms

Also considered

  • Hotel Montelirio (bridge view), Arc de Ronda, Hotel La Fuente de la Higuera (villas of 3/5/8 bedrooms, outside town), Hotel Palacio de Hemingway (center of town, €15/day parking 8 min away)

Alternative: La Melonera villas

4-bed winery villa or 3-bed lodge in the valley — beautiful, private, great for group dinners in, but 10–15 min drive to town every time and you miss the evening ambiance. Depends partly on whether Kiran comes to Ronda. Recommendation: stay in town, visit La Melonera for an afternoon tasting instead.

Good to know

  • Cádiz parking: Muelle Reina Sofía or SABA train-station garage, both <$12/day; Parador has its own garage (~$21)
  • Ronda ZTL: Parador is inside the restricted traffic zone — hotel needs your plate number ahead of time
  • Granada: car returns Dec 24 — after that, taxi up to viewpoints, walk down
  • Dec 25–26: many attractions closed or on holiday hours — plans lean on food, walks, and lights those days
  • Pack: December in Andalucía is mild by day (55–65°F) but cold at night, especially Ronda and Granada — layers and a warm jacket
